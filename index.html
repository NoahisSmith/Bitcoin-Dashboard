Mayer Multiple
Price ÷ 200-day moving average. A simple but reliable gauge of how stretched price is from its own trend. Below 0.6 has historically marked deep-bear accumulation zones; above 2.4 has coincided with cycle-top euphoria. Used as a full-history complement to MVRV Z-Score, whose realized-cap data has no free, keyless, full-history source — the MVRV Z-Score tab adds a ~4-year free series, while Mayer targets the same over/undervaluation idea across all of history using only price data.

Puell Multiple
Daily miner revenue in USD ÷ 365-day MA of daily miner revenue. Miners are the natural sellers of BTC. High Puell values (>4) mean miners are earning unusually high revenue — historically a sell signal. Low values (<0.5) indicate miner capitulation and accumulation opportunities.

MVRV Z-Score
Standardised gap between market cap and realized cap (the aggregate on-chain cost basis): (market − realized) ÷ std(market). Unlike the price-derived metrics, it's a genuinely on-chain valuation signal. Readings above ~7 have marked cycle tops; below ~0.1 have marked bottoms. Sourced precomputed from bitcoin-data.com — keyless but only ~4 years of free history, so earlier dates are blank and it contributes to the risk score only where available.

Composite Risk Score (Historical)
Category-weighted composite — Valuation 45% (200W MA, Mayer, log-regression), Momentum 20% (weekly RSI), Miner 20% (Puell), Sentiment 15% (Fear & Greed). Each metric is scored by its percentile over a trailing ~4-year window (cycle-relative, using only data available at the time), so readings reflect today's regime rather than the 2010–2013 hyper-volatile era, and the historical line carries no hindsight bias. Score 0 = maximum buy opportunity, 10 = maximum sell signal. The first ~2 years are blank while history accumulates.
